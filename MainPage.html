Rethinking Edgar Thomson

This project proposes a future vision over the course of three long-term phases. It maps out a transition to a cleaner industry, and opportunities to enhance the local ecology, restorate land, and to create community infrastructures and open spaces.

Water Catchment Corridor

This project proposes a rainwater catchment corridor and park designs in the south part of Braddock, where there faces a higher probability of flooding with a lower elevation.

Riverfront Trail and Ecological Restoration

This project proposes to enhance the resilience of the Braddock waterfront and riparian space by softening the water and the edge of the city, which brings people and other species to Monongahela River.

Vacant Lot Toolkit

This project explores how small-scale interventions can build ecological networks and transform vacant lots into spaces of care.

Mobile BCLA

Combined with the existing resources and infrastructure of BCLA and the specific needs of residents, this project proposes a mobile module system to help BCLA and related institutions provide a range of community services.

Community Canteen

This project addresses issues of food security and provision in Braddock by retrofitting the former Superior Motors restaurant into a new Community Canteen.

Community Coworking Space

The objective of this project is to extend initiatives and provide further opportunities for creative work, collaboration, and community building among artists, as well as spaces where artists can share their expertise with youth and enhance skill building.

Free Clinic Extention

This project investigates gaps within the health landscape of Braddock, and proposes creating connections to build a stronger network between existing facilities and the community.
